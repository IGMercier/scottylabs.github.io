Partners

Carnegie Mellon School of Computer Science

The Carnegie Mellon School of Computer Science Undergraduate Program has provided space for ScottyLabs to host events in the beautiful Gates-Hillman Building. SCS has also provided administrative, organizational, and logistic help for our events; TartanHacks would not be possible without their help. SCS is also the home school for many ScottyLabs members. Find out more at www.cs.cmu.edu.

Project Olympus

Project Olympus provides advice, resources, and connections for Carnegie Mellon students and faculty who want to start companies. Olympus provides resources for ScottyLabs projects that want to explore their commercial potential, and also provides organizational help with ScottyLabs events.

ACM@CMU

ACM@CMU is dedicated to advancing technology and computing across campus. We are passionate about developing applications that benefit the Carnegie Mellon community at large, and publish a monthly tech newsletter in which we interview students, professors, and report on the latest technologies. On the professional side of things, we work with employers to provide students with the best networking and employment opportunities. We also play hard; from pickup Frisbee games to Kinect parties, our members stay busy.

Mobile Apps Club

Mobile Apps Club provides resources and guidance for mobile developers at Carnegie Mellon. Our introductory workshops provide a way for aspiring developers to get started with mobile technologies, and our device rental program allows developers of all experience levels to test their apps across multiple devices. More recently, we've worked with School of Computer Science faculty to create 15-237, a brand new course on cross-platform mobile web development.

PennApps

PennApps is the largest student-run hackathon in the country. PennApps inspired our hackathon, TartanHacks.
